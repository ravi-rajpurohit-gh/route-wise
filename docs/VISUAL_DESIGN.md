# RouteWise Visual Design Direction

## Objective

Make RouteWise look and behave like a credible retail-operations analysis
product, not a generic SaaS landing page or speculative consumer application.

## Problems identified in the first visual slice

- An oversized marketing slogan occupied the most valuable screen area.
- Neon-on-dark styling and glow effects prioritized novelty over clarity.
- Decorative controls, including a fake profile, implied unsupported features.
- The baseline, measured result, and limitations were visually secondary.
- Rounded cards and badges lacked a consistent information hierarchy.

## Design principles

1. **Evidence before promotion:** Lead with the route comparison, baseline, and
   caveat rather than a slogan.
2. **Operational clarity:** Use restrained color, compact typography, and
   deliberate borders suitable for repeated analytical use.
3. **No product fiction:** Do not imply authentication, live GPS, or retailer
   integrations that do not exist.
4. **Visible methodology:** Show the algorithm, active constraints, synthetic
   data status, and estimate limitations.
5. **Meaningful color:** Blue identifies the optimized route, amber identifies
   the baseline, and yellow marks high-value evidence or entry context.

## First refinement pass

- Replaced the marketing hero with a route-analysis header.
- Added a four-part evidence summary above the workspace.
- Reworked the floor plan into a light technical drawing.
- Made optimized and baseline strategies visually distinct.
- Added explicit simulation and estimate caveats.
- Reframed the item list as an operational pick sequence.

## Acceptance criteria

- A reviewer can identify the problem, baseline, result, and limitation without
  scrolling.
- Switching route strategies clearly changes both sequence and map treatment.
- The interface remains usable at desktop and mobile widths.
- Visual changes do not alter optimizer behavior or reported calculations.

## Remaining work

- Document desktop, tablet, and mobile screenshots.
- Add complete keyboard focus treatment.
- Run automated accessibility checks.
- Validate terminology and density with fulfillment users.
